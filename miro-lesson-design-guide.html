Step-by-Step Walkthrough

Design Your
Miro Mini-Session

A guided workflow that takes you from a blank idea to a fully structured session — with the right Miro technique at every stage.

① Define ② Choose ③ Sequence ④ Build ⑤ Facilitate

Phase 01

Define Your Session

⏱ 10–15 min

Before touching Miro, get clear on your purpose. Every design decision follows from these three anchors.

🎯

Step 1

Set your learning objective

Write one sentence that completes this prompt: "By the end of this session, learners will be able to ___." Use an action verb — analyse, create, evaluate, map, prioritise. This sentence is the north star for every choice you make.

👥

Step 2

Know your group

Your technique choices depend on who's in the room. A group of 4 can hold a rich Concept Mapping discussion; a group of 40 needs Breakout Frames to stay manageable. Answer these before moving on.

🧩

Step 3

Identify the thinking mode

What cognitive work do you need learners to do? Match your session to one of these modes — or a combination for longer sessions.

💡
Generate
Produce many ideas; divergent thinking
🔗
Connect
See relationships; build mental models
📦
Organise
Sort, cluster, and label information
⚖️
Decide
Prioritise and converge on choices

Phase 02

Choose Your Technique(s)

⏱ 5–10 min

Match the thinking mode you identified to the Miro technique built for it. For sessions over 30 minutes, combine 2–3 techniques.

🗺 Technique Selector — tap to mark what fits your session

🗒️
Sticky Note Storm
Generate → diverge → open a session. Best for 5+ min openers, any group size.
🗂️
Affinity Mapping
Organise → synthesise → mid-session. Follows a brainstorm naturally.
🗺️
Concept Mapping
Connect → small groups → complex topics. Best for 15–45 min deep dives.
🗳️
Dot Voting
Decide → converge → close a session. 3–10 min; any size.
🖼️
Breakout Frames
Structure → parallel groups → large sessions. Use as container for other techniques.

⚡ Matching rule of thumb

One technique fits a 10–20 min mini-session. Two techniques (e.g. Storm + Vote) suit 25–35 min. Three techniques (Storm → Affinity → Vote) fill a 40–60 min session with natural momentum.

⚠️ First-time Miro users? Stick to one technique and keep it simple. Familiarity with the canvas matters more than variety — learners who spend 10 minutes figuring out how to add a sticky note won't engage with your content.

Sequencing principles

The diamond rule

Every good session follows a diamond shape: expand first, then contract. Diverge before you converge. Never open with a decision or close with a brainstorm.

Phase 04

Build the Board

⏱ 20–45 min

Now you go into Miro. Work from structure to content — never the other way around.

🏗️

Step A

Set up your frame structure first

Create one frame per section of the session. Name each frame clearly. Frames give learners a sense of place on the infinite canvas and let you use Miro's "Present" mode for a guided walkthrough.

🎨

Step B

Add prompts and scaffolding

Inside each frame, add the instructions learners will read while you're talking. Keep them short. Use large text for the main question and smaller text for instructions. Don't overcrowd — white space reduces cognitive load.

🖼️ Using Breakout Frames? Add a shared Parking Lot frame outside all group areas for off-topic ideas.
🔒

Step C

Lock what shouldn't move

Any element you don't want learners to accidentally drag — headers, instructions, example cards, cluster labels — should be locked. Right-click → Lock. This single habit prevents the most common session disruptions.

🧪

Step D

Test as a learner

Open the board in a private / incognito window and do everything a learner would do. Add a sticky note, drag it, vote, navigate between frames. If anything feels confusing for 30 seconds, redesign it — your learners will have the same confusion in real time.

Phase 05

Facilitate the Session

⏱ During session

You've planned it — now run it. These moves separate a good facilitator from a great one.

🚀

Opening

Orient before you activate

Never jump straight to the activity. Spend 2 minutes orienting: share the board link, show where things are, model adding one sticky note yourself. A 2-minute orientation saves 10 minutes of confusion mid-session.

⏰

During

Time-keep visibly and narrate transitions

Use Miro's built-in timer for timed activities and announce when you move frames. Silence on a shared canvas is uncomfortable — narrate what you see: "I can see ideas coming in from Team B — great, keep going."

🎤

Debrief

Connect the board back to the objective

The board is a means, not the end. In the debrief, zoom out (Ctrl+Shift+H), show the full session canvas, and tie what you see back to the learning objective stated at the start. Ask learners: "What surprised you? What would you add?"

💡 Post-session habit

After each session, spend 5 minutes on the board: screenshot the final state, note one thing that worked and one thing to change, then save the board as a template. Over time you'll build a library of reusable session designs.
